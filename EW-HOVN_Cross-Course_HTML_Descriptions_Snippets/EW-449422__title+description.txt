🧸 HSI Pediatric First Aid | CPR AED (Blended)
Be prepared. Stay calm. Take action. This nationally recognized HSI Pediatric First Aid | CPR AED – Blended (Guidelines 2020) course gives childcare professionals and families the skills to respond quickly to infant, child, and adult emergencies. The blended format combines convenient online learning with a brief, hands-on in-person skills session for real-world confidence and compliance.

🖥️ Part 1 – Online: Interactive modules you can complete on your schedule (mobile/desktop friendly)
🏥 Part 2 – Skills: Short, instructor-led practice and skills verification with manikins and AED trainer
✅ 2-year HSI certification upon successful completion
💼 Ideal for licensed childcare, preschools, daycares, after-school programs, camps, babysitters, nannies, foster/adoptive parents, and youth-program staff

📚 What You’ll Learn

🤒 Medical emergencies: asthma, difficulty breathing, seizures, diabetic issues, severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
🩹 Injury care: bleeding control, bandaging, burns, sprains/strains, fractures, head/neck/spine precautions
😮‍💨 Choking relief techniques for infants, children, and adults
💓 High-quality CPR (infant/child/adult) and safe AED operation
🌊 Drowning and near-drowning response; post-rescue care
🐍 Bites, stings, and environmental exposures (heat/cold, poison)
🧠 Prevention spotlight: Shaken Baby Syndrome/Abusive Head Trauma

📝 Compliance at a Glance (NC DCDEE 10A NCAC 09 .1102)
This course fulfills the CPR/First Aid certification piece and teaches several emergency topics child-care staff need. For full .1102 compliance, add the NC-approved trainings and your center’s written policies listed below.

✓ Pediatric CPR & First Aid (infant/child)
✓ Prevention/response to food allergies & anaphylaxis
✓ Shaken Baby Syndrome / Abusive Head Trauma (prevention)
□ Infectious-disease control & immunization program policies
□ Medication administration & parental-consent standards
□ Building/premises safety (electrical, water, traffic hazards)
□ Emergency Preparedness & Response (written EPR plan + training)
□ Hazardous-materials handling & biocontaminant disposal
□ Child transportation precautions
□ Recognizing/reporting child abuse, neglect, maltreatment
□ SIDS prevention & safe sleep (ITS-SIDS)

Finish the checklist: NC requires ITS-SIDS Safe Sleep, Emergency Preparedness & Response (EPR), Medication Administration, and Recognizing/Reporting Child Maltreatment trainings, plus your center’s written policies for premises safety, hazards/biowaste, transportation, and immunizations.
