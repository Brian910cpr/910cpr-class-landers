First Aid CPR AED Certification – AHA Heartsaver In-Person Class

The American Heart Association Heartsaver First Aid CPR AED course is an instructor-led classroom program for people who need first aid and CPR certification for work, regulatory compliance, or general preparedness.

This class teaches participants how to respond to medical emergencies, injury emergencies, choking, and sudden cardiac arrest using adult, child, and infant CPR and AED skills.

Who This Course Is For

  • Workplace safety responders
  • Teachers and school staff
  • Coaches and scout leaders
  • Construction and industrial employees
  • Non-medical home care workers
  • Anyone required to have OSHA-aligned first aid and CPR training

What You Will Learn

  • First aid basics and scene assessment
  • Medical emergencies
  • Injury emergencies
  • Adult, child, and infant CPR
  • Relief of choking
  • Use of an Automated External Defibrillator (AED)

Course Format

This is a traditional instructor-led classroom course with hands-on skills practice and testing.

Students complete all training in one visit, making this a practical option for people who want a straightforward in-person certification experience.

Certification

Students who successfully complete the course receive a 2-year American Heart Association Heartsaver First Aid CPR AED completion card.

Workplace Compliance

This course is commonly used for workplace responders and aligns with OSHA expectations for employees who may need to respond to first aid or cardiac emergencies as part of their job duties.

Why This Course Matters

For many students, this is the simplest path: one class, one visit, one certification card covering first aid, CPR, and AED.
