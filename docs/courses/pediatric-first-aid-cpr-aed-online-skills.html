Pediatric First Aid CPR AED Certification – AHA Heartsaver (Online + Skills)

The American Heart Association Heartsaver Pediatric First Aid CPR AED course prepares caregivers, teachers, and childcare professionals to respond confidently to emergencies involving infants and children.

This blended learning format allows students to complete the knowledge portion online at their own pace, followed by a short in-person skills session to demonstrate hands-on CPR and first aid techniques.

Who This Course Is For

  • Childcare providers and daycare staff
  • Teachers and school employees
  • Summer camp staff
  • Youth sports coaches
  • Foster parents and guardians
  • Babysitters and nannies
  • Parents and grandparents

What You Will Learn

  • Pediatric illness recognition and response
  • Bleeding control and injury care
  • Allergic reactions and epinephrine use
  • Asthma emergencies
  • Bites, stings, and burns
  • Drowning response
  • Infant and child CPR
  • Use of an Automated External Defibrillator (AED)
  • Relief of choking

Course Format

This course uses the American Heart Association blended learning format.

  • Part 1: Online self-paced training covering pediatric first aid and CPR concepts
  • Part 2: In-person skills session with an instructor

The hands-on session focuses on practicing CPR, AED use, and choking relief skills for infants and children.

Certification

Students who successfully complete both parts receive an American Heart Association Heartsaver Pediatric First Aid CPR AED completion card valid for two years.

Regulatory Use

This course is commonly used to meet childcare provider CPR and first aid training requirements in many states, including programs regulated under childcare licensing agencies.
