BLS Certification for Healthcare Providers

The American Heart Association Basic Life Support (BLS) Provider course is designed for healthcare professionals who need CPR certification for clinical work.

This instructor-led class teaches high-quality CPR, AED use, and team-based resuscitation skills used in hospitals, clinics, and emergency medical settings.

Who This Course Is For

  • Nurses and nursing students
  • EMTs and paramedics
  • Medical and dental professionals
  • Respiratory therapists
  • Physical therapists and medical staff
  • Healthcare students and clinical trainees

Course Topics

  • Adult, child, and infant CPR
  • Automated External Defibrillator (AED) use
  • Team-based resuscitation and communication
  • Airway management and ventilation techniques
  • Recognition of cardiac arrest and respiratory emergencies

Course Format

  • Instructor-led classroom training
  • Hands-on skills practice
  • Skills testing using AHA guidelines

Classes typically take 3–4 hours to complete depending on class size.

Certification

Students who successfully complete the course receive a 2-year American Heart Association BLS Provider eCard.

This certification is accepted by hospitals, healthcare employers, and medical training programs nationwide.
